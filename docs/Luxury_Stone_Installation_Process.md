**Luxury Stone Installation Process in Australia**

---

### 1. Material Handling & Site Access

**Site protection:**
Before bringing the stone inside, installers must cover floors (timber, tiles) with protective sheets or mats and apply corner guards to walls and door frames to prevent scratches during handling.

**Safe transportation:**
Luxury stone slabs are extremely heavy and prone to cracking if handled incorrectly. Installers use A-frame trolleys, slab lifters, and professional suction cups to safely transport large slabs into the kitchen.

**Visual inspection:**
Ensure the slab is free from cracks, chipped edges, or surface scratches after transport from the factory to the site.

---

### 2. Site Checking & Dry-Fitting

**Cabinet inspection:**
Use a spirit level or laser level to confirm that the kitchen cabinets are perfectly level and structurally sound. Uneven cabinets may lead to stone cracking over time.

**Dry-fit:**
Place the slabs on the cabinets without adhesive to recheck dimensions: wall alignment, sink cut-outs, and cooktop openings must match perfectly with on-site conditions.

**On-site adjustments:**
If walls are uneven (very common), installers will slightly trim or scribe the stone edges to ensure a tight fit.
*Note:* In Australia, on-site cutting is strictly regulated due to silica dust risks. Wet cutting methods and industrial vacuum systems are mandatory.

---

### 3. Material Preparation (Adhesives, Silicone, Accessories)

**Epoxy resin mixing:**
This step reflects craftsmanship. Installers mix and color-match epoxy resin to blend seamlessly with the stone’s veining, aiming for nearly invisible joints (seams).

**Silicone preparation:**
Structural silicone is used to bond the stone to the cabinetry, while mold-resistant silicone is applied around sinks and splashback areas.

---

### 4. Installation & Joining

**Applying adhesive/silicone:**
Apply silicone in a wave pattern along the cabinet edges to provide flexible adhesion.

**Placing and joining slabs:**
Carefully position slabs in place. For long runs or L/U-shaped kitchens, epoxy is applied along the joining edges.

**Using seam setters:**
A mandatory tool. Vacuum seam setters pull slabs tightly together and level them precisely, ensuring joints are flush and smooth without lippage.

---

### 5. Finishing & Cleaning

**Removing excess adhesive:**
Once the epoxy reaches a semi-cured state, installers scrape off excess and clean the surface using acetone or specialized cleaners.

**Sealing:**
For natural stones (e.g., marble, granite), a protective sealer is applied to prevent staining from substances like wine, oil, lemon, or coffee.

**Site cleanup:**
Clean the entire work area, remove debris, vacuum dust, and polish the benchtop. In Australia, leaving the site spotless is a mandatory service standard.

---

### 6. Handover & Client Walkthrough

**Final inspection with client:**
The lead installer walks through the finished installation with the client, checking joint smoothness, surface finish, and overall quality.

**Care & maintenance guidance:**
Advise clients on daily cleaning (use pH-neutral products) and precautions such as avoiding direct chopping or placing hot cookware directly on the surface.

**Handover:**
Take photos/videos for portfolio purposes, complete handover documentation, and activate the warranty.
